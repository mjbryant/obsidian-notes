## Instructions

1. Combine roasted peanut oil, rice vinegar, garlic, sugar, salt, and black pepper in a small blender or food processor.
2. Blend until smooth and the garlic is fully incorporated. Set vinaigrette aside.
3. Chop the cilantro, green onions, peanuts, and fresno peppers.
4. Working in batches, pulse the kale and cabbage in a food processor until very finely chopped. Stop before the greens become mushy.
5. If the greens release excess water, gently squeeze them with a paper towel.
6. Transfer the chopped greens to a large bowl.
7. Add a portion of the vinaigrette to the greens and massage with your hands for about one minute until the kale is tender.
8. Add the peanuts, fresno peppers, cilantro, and green onions to the bowl.
9. Drizzle over the remaining vinaigrette and toss to combine.
10. Serve immediately.

## Notes

- The vinaigrette can also be made without a blender: grate the garlic directly into a jar with the remaining dressing ingredients and shake well to combine.
- Massaging the kale with the dressing is important — it softens the texture and mellows any bitterness.
- The finely chopped greens allow the vinaigrette to coat every nook and cranny of the salad.
- Salad is best served fresh but the chopped greens can be prepped ahead and stored separately from the dressing.
