## Instructions

1. Wash and dry all produce.
2. Peel and mince or grate the garlic.
3. Trim green beans and cut in half if necessary.
4. Quarter the lemon.
5. Roughly chop the parsley.
6. Heat a drizzle of oil in a medium pan over medium-high heat.
7. Add chicken in a single layer and season with thyme, salt, and pepper.
8. Cook, stirring occasionally, until browned, 1–2 minutes.
9. Add garlic and couscous. Cook, stirring, until garlic is fragrant and couscous is toasted, 30–60 seconds.
10. Stir in mushroom stock concentrate, 1¾ cups water (2¼ cups for 4 servings), and a pinch of salt.
11. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and cook until chicken is cooked through and water is absorbed, 12–14 minutes.
12. Keep covered off heat.
13. Heat a drizzle of oil in a large pan over medium-high heat.
14. Add green beans and season with salt and pepper.
15. Cook, stirring occasionally, until browned and tender, 3–6 minutes.
16. Squeeze juice from two lemon wedges over the chicken and couscous.
17. Divide between plates.
18. Serve topped with green beans, garnished with parsley, and with remaining lemon wedges on the side.

## Notes

- Roll the lemon under your palm on the countertop before quartering to soften the fruit and make it easier to extract juice.
- For 4 servings, use a large pan; cooking in batches may be necessary for proteins and vegetables.
- Israeli couscous (contains wheat) — check for allergens before serving.
- Nutrition per serving (2-serving recipe): ~540 calories, 11g fat, 2g saturated fat, 590mg sodium, 71g carbohydrates, 6g dietary fiber, 38g protein.
