## Instructions

1. Wash and dry all produce. Peel and mince or grate the garlic. Trim green beans if necessary and cut in half. Quarter the lemon. Roughly chop the parsley.
2. Open the package of chicken and drain off any excess liquid. Heat a drizzle of olive oil in a medium pan over medium-high heat.
3. Add chicken in a single layer and season with thyme, salt, and pepper. Cook, stirring occasionally, until browned, 1–2 minutes. Do not stir while cooking initially in this step.
4. Add garlic and couscous to the pan. Stir until garlic is fragrant and couscous is toasted, 30–60 seconds.
5. Stir in the mushroom stock concentrate, 1¾ cups water (2¼ cups for 4 servings), and a pinch of salt. Cover and bring to a boil.
6. Reduce heat to low and cook until chicken is cooked through and couscous is tender and water has absorbed, 12–14 minutes. Keep covered off heat.
7. Heat a drizzle of olive oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Add green beans and season with salt and pepper.
8. Cook green beans, stirring occasionally, until browned and tender, 3–6 minutes.
9. Squeeze juice from two lemon wedges over the chicken and couscous. Garnish with parsley.
10. Divide between plates. Serve with green beans and remaining lemon wedges.

## Notes

- Roll the lemon under your palm on the countertop before quartering to soften the citrus fruit and make it easier to get all the juice when squeezing.
- To make 3 servings, lighten up on the seasoning and any other ingredients you like; this keeps the balance just right.
- To make 6 servings, triple the 2-serving recipe amounts.
