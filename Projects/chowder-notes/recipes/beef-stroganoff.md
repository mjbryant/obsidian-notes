## Instructions

1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Trim and thinly slice mushrooms (skip if pre-sliced). Peel, halve, and dice the onion. Peel and mince garlic. Thinly slice chives.
2. Once water is boiling, add linguine to pot. Cook, stirring occasionally, until al dente, 9–11 minutes. Reserve ¾ cup pasta cooking water, then drain and return linguine to pot.
3. While pasta cooks, pat steak dry with paper towels. Thinly slice against the grain. Place steak in a medium bowl and season all over with salt and pepper. Add half the flour and toss to coat.
4. Heat a drizzle of oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Add coated steak to pan and cook until browned all over, 2–3 minutes. Transfer steak to a plate. Turn off heat. (For 4 servings, cook in batches if necessary.)
5. Heat a drizzle of oil in the pan used for steak over medium-high heat. Add mushrooms and cook, stirring occasionally, until browned and tender, 4–6 minutes.
6. Reduce heat to medium. Add garlic and 2 tbsp butter (2 tbsp for 4 servings). Cook until fragrant, 30–60 seconds. Add remaining flour and cook, stirring, 30 seconds. Add cream sauce mixture and cook, stirring, until slightly thickened, 2–3 minutes.
7. Meanwhile, in a second medium bowl, combine cream sauce base, half the stock concentrate, worcestershire sauce, mustard, half the cream sauce base, and 1 cup water (2 cups for 4 servings).
8. In a small bowl, combine sour cream and 2 tbsp stroganoff sauce (4 tbsp for 4 servings) from the pan. This step prevents sour cream from separating. Stir tempered sour cream mixture into pan and keep covered until ready to serve.
9. Return steak to pan with veggies and sauce. Stir, cooking until steak is cooked through, 1–2 minutes. Remove from heat.
10. To pot with drained linguine, add half the reserved pasta cooking water and 3 tbsp butter (6 tbsp for 4 servings). Cook over medium-high heat until butter has melted and sauce evenly coats pasta, 2–3 minutes.
11. Divide pasta between shallow bowls. Top with stroganoff. Season with salt and pepper. Garnish with remaining chives and serve.

## Notes

- Temper the sour cream before adding it to the hot pan to prevent curdling.
- Reserve pasta cooking water before draining — the starch helps the sauce coat the pasta.
- Cook steak in batches for 4 servings to ensure proper browning rather than steaming.
- Beef is fully cooked when internal temperature reaches 145°F.
