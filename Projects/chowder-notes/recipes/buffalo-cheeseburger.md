## Instructions

1. Adjust rack to middle position and preheat oven to 450 degrees.
2. Wash and dry the potatoes.
3. Cut potatoes into ½-inch-thick wedges and place on a baking sheet.
4. Toss wedges with a big drizzle of oil, half the garlic powder, and a large pinch of salt and pepper.
5. Roast on middle rack until browned and tender, 20–25 minutes.
6. While potatoes roast, combine sour cream, ranch dressing, and remaining garlic powder in a small bowl.
7. Season sauce with salt and pepper. Set aside some sauce for serving.
8. In a large bowl, combine beef, Frank's seasoning, a dash of hot sauce, and salt. Mix to combine.
9. Form mixture into two patties, each slightly wider than a burger bun.
10. Heat a large drizzle of oil in a large pan over medium heat.
11. Add patties and cook until browned and cooked to desired doneness, 3–5 minutes per side.
12. In the last 1–2 minutes of cooking, top patties with Monterey Jack cheese. Cover pan to melt.
13. Halve and toast the burger buns.
14. Spread cut sides of buns with creamy ranch sauce.
15. Fill buns with patties and crispy fried onions.
16. Divide burgers between plates and serve with potato wedges and remaining sauce on the side.

## Notes

- Try not to overwork the meat when forming patties. A light touch when shaping keeps the balance just right.
- Using a light touch when shaping will make for juicier results.
- Add a dash of hot sauce to the sauce if you like things spicy — but save some plain sauce for forming patties.
- For 4 servings, form four patties and triple all other ingredients.
