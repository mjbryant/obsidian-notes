## Instructions

1. Warm olive oil in a heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat. Add garlic and rosemary and cook until the garlic is fragrant and just starting to color, about 2 minutes. Remove the rosemary sprig.
2. Add crushed tomatoes and stir to combine. Cook 3 minutes.
3. Add beans, broth, and parmesan rind if using. Bring to a simmer and cook uncovered for 20 minutes.
4. Use the back of a wooden spoon or a ladle to mash roughly half the beans against the side of the pot. Stir to integrate — the soup should thicken noticeably.
5. Add pasta. Cook until al dente per package directions, stirring occasionally. The soup will thicken further as pasta absorbs broth; add a splash of water if needed.
6. Taste and adjust salt and pepper. Remove parmesan rind before serving.

## Notes

Better the second day. If making ahead, cook the pasta separately and add it when reheating — otherwise it absorbs all the broth overnight. A drizzle of good olive oil at the table is not optional.
