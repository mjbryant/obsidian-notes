## Sprites
### aesprite 
Toggle “pixel-perfect” to get rid of “doubles”, the secondary pixels that make things chunky

## Colors
#### Palettes
[lospec.com](https://lospec.com/) to get a palette. Advice is to not try to construct your own palette

#### Shading
Three colors per material, as a general rule: base color, shadow, highlight. The shadow and highlight don’t need to just be darker/lighter versions of the base color. They can be ”analogous colors” (google it). Don’t use too many colors.

First do base color, then shadows, the highlights. Think about where the light source is coming from, and keep the source consistent for all sprites.
## Textures
“Dithering” is like a checkerboard pattern between two colors. It can be used to show textures on an otherwise boring surface.
## Outlines
Don’t have to use a black outline! It’s normally a darker version of the color of the object.

You can also play around with different outlines for different objects to indicate that they’re special. Like in Celeste only objects you can interact with have a black outline.
