## Instructions

1. Adjust oven rack to middle position and preheat oven to 425°F.
2. Line a baking sheet with foil and brush with oil or coat with nonstick spray.
3. Wash and dry produce.
4. Peel and thinly slice the onion. Mince a few slices until you have 1 tbsp (2 tbsp for 4 servings). Set aside minced onion for topping.
5. Heat a drizzle of oil in a large pan over medium-high heat.
6. Add sliced onion and cook for 3 minutes until softened.
7. Add ground beef, breaking into pieces. Cook until browned, 4–6 minutes.
8. Add Tex-Mex paste and Southwest Spice Blend. Stir to combine.
9. Add water (¼ cup for 2 servings). Simmer until mixture has thickened and beef is cooked through, 2–4 minutes.
10. Turn off heat.
11. Drizzle tortillas with 1 tbsp olive oil (2 tbsp for 4 servings) on a clean work surface. Brush or rub to coat completely.
12. Add a heaping ¼ cup of beef filling to one side of each tortilla.
13. Sprinkle each filled tortilla with Mexican cheese blend.
14. Roll up tortillas starting with the filled side, seam sides down, and place on prepared baking sheet.
15. Make sure flautas are snug on the sheet to prevent unrolling.
16. Bake on middle rack until golden brown and crispy, 8–12 minutes.
17. While flautas bake, finely dice tomato. In a small bowl, combine tomato and minced onion. Season with salt.
18. Divide flautas between plates.
19. Top with pico de gallo and red pepper crema. Serve.

## Notes

- Insert a toothpick through each flauta before baking to keep them from unrolling; remove before eating.
- To make 3 servings, triple the 2-serving recipe.
- Ground meat should be cooked to an internal temperature of 160°F.
- Consuming raw or undercooked meat increases risk of foodborne illness.
